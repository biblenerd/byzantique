The name Jairus is a possible allusion to Jair ([Judges 10:3–5](ref:JDG 10:3-5)). Interestingly, Jair was a Gileadite (the land Jesus just returned from),[^gilead] and the next judge, Jephthah, has some interesting parallels/reversals to this account (albeit, it feel like a stretch but I found it interesting nonetheless).

Heiser noted similarities between Jephthah's tragedy and Jesus' raising of Jairus' daughter in this pericope.[^heiser] Notable examples include:

- Name similarity: Jair vs. Jairus (except that the main connection is actually with Jepthah).

- Gilead connection: Jair (and Jepthah) was a Gileadite, Jesus just returned from ancient Gilead.

- Only daughter: Both Jephthah's and Jairus' only daughter is the one who dies.

- Reversal of outcome: Whereas Jephthah's daughter dies due to his own foolishness, Jesus raises Jairus' daughter from the dead.


[^gilead]: See [note on Luke 8:26](note:LUK_08_26-gadarenes).
[^heiser]: Michael Heiser, *I dare you not to bore me with the Bible* (Bellingham, WA: Lexham Press, 2014), locs. 637–681 (Kindle edition).
