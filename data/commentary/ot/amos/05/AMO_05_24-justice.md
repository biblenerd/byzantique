The prophets reserved their harshest critiques for God's people, while also pronouncing judgment on the surrounding nations. Amos indicated that because of its injustices, God despised and rejected His people's liturgical practices, chastising them in v. 24. These demonstrate God's beating heart for justice and mercy in a world that is filled with violence and oppression.
