The Hebrew שֹׁאָה֙ (*shoa*) in this passage is the basis for modern Jewish references to "Holocaust." The corresponding translation in ENGLXXUP is "desolation" from the Greek ἀωρία (*aōria*). Brenton's original translation has a textual footnote indicating this word could be translated literally as "unseasonableness."[^lsj]


[^lsj]: [LSJ](https://lsj.gr/wiki/%E1%BC%80%CF%89%CF%81%CE%AF%CE%B1) has additional glosses: "wrong time" and "untimely fate or death."
