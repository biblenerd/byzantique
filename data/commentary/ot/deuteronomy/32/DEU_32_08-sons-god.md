title: Angels or sons? Of God or Israel?
tags: source-criticism, translation
---

There are essentially three English variant readings for the final words of this passage:

- "angels of God" ([ENGLXXUP](/about/texts-and-translations/#englxxup))
- "sons of Israel" (MT)
- "sons of God" (DSS)

Here's a comparison of the main textual traditions and specific readings in each:

| Source Text | Reading | Translation |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| MT (BHS) | בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל (*bənê yiśrāʾēl*) | "sons of Israel" |
| DSS (4QDeutᶦ/ᑫ) | בְּנֵי אֵל / הָאֱלֹהִים (*bənê ʾēl / hāʾĕlōhîm*) | "sons of El/God" |
| Göttingen LXX | υἱῶν θεοῦ (*huiōn theou*) | "sons of God" |
| Majority LXX<br />[(GRCBRENT)](/about/texts-and-translations/#grcbrent) | ἀγγέλων θεοῦ (*angelōn theou*) | "angels of God" |
| Harmonized LXX | υἱῶν Ἰσραήλ (*huiōn Israēl*) | "sons of Israel" |

It is likely that "sons of God" is the most primitive reading. The "angels of God" reading is probably a later theological clarification.[^heiser]


[^heiser]: *Cf., e.g.,* Michael S. Heiser, "Deuteronomy 32:8 and the Sons of God," *Bibliotheca Sacra*, no. 158 (January–March 2001): 52–74 [(drmsh.com)](https://drmsh.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/Heiser-Deuteronomy-32-8-and-the-sons-of-God.pdf).
