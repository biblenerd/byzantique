The Hebrew MT has 3 pairs of attributes (for a total of 6) describing the Spirit of God, whereas the Greek LXX has 7. The "fear of God" is also mentioned twice in the MT (vv. 2-3), but only once in the LXX.

The spirit of:

| Greek LXX | ENGLXXUP | Hebrew MT | WEB |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----|
| σοφίας καὶ συνέσεως<br />(*sophias kai syneseōs*) | "wisdom and understanding" | חָכְמָ֣ה וּבִינָ֗ה <br />(*ḥokmâ ûbînâ*) | "wisdom and understanding" |
| βουλῆς καὶ ἰσχύος<br />(*boulēs kai ischyos*) | "counsel and strength" | ר֤וּחַ עֵצָה֙ וּגְבוּרָ֔ה <br />(*rûaḥ ʿēṣâ ûgəbûrâ*) | "counsel and might" |
| γνώσεως καὶ εὐσεβείας ἐμπλήσει αὐτὸν<br />(*gnōseōs kai eusebeias emplēsei auton*) | "knowledge and godliness shall fill him" | דַּ֖עַת (*daʿat*) | "knowledge" |
| φόβου Θεοῦ<br />(*phobou THeou*) | "the fear of God" | יִרְאַ֥ת יְהוָֽה <br />(*yirʾat yhwh*) | "fear of Yahweh" |

The LXX reading provides the basis for the Latin scholastic understanding of 7 gifts of the Holy Spirit.

>Later still it will provide the textual basis for the seven gifts of the Holy Spirit (*sacrum septenarium*) developed in the West by Augustine, Gregory the Great, and Thomas Aquinas on the basis of LXX, which adds piety (*eusebeia* cf. Vulg. *spiritus pietatis*) to make seven.[^ayb]
