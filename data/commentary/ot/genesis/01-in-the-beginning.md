The Septuagint opens with **ἐν ἀρχῇ** (*en archē*), "in the beginning." The word *archē*
is not merely the first tick of a clock; it carries the sense of an originating
**principle** — that from which all else proceeds and on which it depends.

> The Fathers do not read "beginning" as a bare point in time, but as the source and
> rationale of created things.

Read in the light of the New Testament, the Church hears this *archē* christologically:

{{ JHN 1:1 }}

"The heaven and the earth," meanwhile, is a Hebrew way of naming **the whole cosmos** — the
visible and the invisible together — not two items in a list but a totality.
