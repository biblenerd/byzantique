---
anchor: GEN 3:20
title: Adam names Eve
tags: translation, gender, anthropology
---

Whereas Adam named the creatures he ruled over beginning in [2:20](ref:GEN 2:20), he had not named his wife because she was his equal.[^chrysostom] However, now that they have fallen and Eve has been subjected to Adam, he now names her as he previously did the animals. 

Her name points to hope for how humanity will now continue through childbearing, albeit with pain and suffering. I herein refer to the traditional name "Eve" (חַוָּה / *Havvah*) despite the LXX (appropriately) using Ζωή (*Zōē*, meaning "Life") in this verse. She is later called Eve (Εὕαν) beginning in [4:1](ref:GEN 4:1) (LXX). The Hebrew חַוָּה (*Havvah*) was etymologically related to חָֽי (*ḥāy*, "the living"), which is retained in the Greek LXX: Ζωή (*Zōē* = "Life") is the mother of the ζώντων (*zoōntōn* = "living").


[^chrysostom]: *Cf.* St. John Chrysostom, *Homilies on Genesis* 17.36.
