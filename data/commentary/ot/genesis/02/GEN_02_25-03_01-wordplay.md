anchor: GEN 2:25-3:1
title: Nude ... shrewd
tags: wordplay, translation
---

There is wordplay in the Hebrew connecting [2:25](ref:GEN 2:25) with [3:1](ref:GEN 3:1). "And the two of them were naked (עֲרוּמִּ֔ים / *arummim*, which is the plural form of *arum*)…. Now the serpent was shrewd (עָר֔וּם / *arum*)…."

>The pun ties together the creation of Adam and Eve and their temptation, and it points to the futility of disobedience: "The nude humans have been duped by the shrewd serpent; they want to be shrewd, but in the end they are only nude…."
>
>Although "nude" would make the pun more obvious in English, "naked" is the right word because of its association with vulnerability and shame.[^bray]


[^bray]: Samuel L. Bray and John F. Hobbins, *Genesis 1-11: A New Old Translation for Readers, Scholars, and Translators* (Wilmore, KY: GlossaHouse, 2017), 108.
