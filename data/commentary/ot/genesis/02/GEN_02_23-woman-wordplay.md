The Hebrew contains etymological wordplay with "man" (אִישׁ / *ish*) and *woman* (אִשָּׁה / *ishah*), which is generally also present in English (man & wo***man***). Brenton's translation ([ENGLXXUP](/about/texts-and-translations/#englxxup))  doesn't show this *Hebrew* wordplay (woman... husband), but "husband" is a faithful translation of the *Greek* LXX's ἀνδρὸς (*andros*).
