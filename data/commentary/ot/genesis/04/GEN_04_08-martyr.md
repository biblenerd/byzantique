Abel is the first martyr since he is killed for his faith and sacrifice to God (*cf.* [Matthew 25:35](ref:MAT 25:35); [Hebrews 11:4](ref:HEB 11:4)), and the first human to die is killed by murder.

{{ 1JN 3:12 }}

The word "brother" appears exactly 7 times between vv. [2–11](ref:GEN 4:2-11) (both in the in the Hebrew and the Greek text).

The Hebrew is awkward and is missing whatever Cain said to Abel. The LXX (along with several other ancient translations) has added "Let us go out into the plain" (Διέλθωμεν εἰς τὸ πεδίον / *Dielthōmen eis to pedion*). Some targums contain a discussion between the brothers about God prior to Abel's murder.
