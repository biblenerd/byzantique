The Hebrew name "Abel" (הֶבֶל / *Hevel*) means "breath," "vapor," or "vanity." This is the same word used throughout the book of Ecclesiastes (in Hebrew) which is frequently translated as "vanity" (*cf., e.g.,* [Ecclesiastes 1:2](ref:ECC 1:2)).[^halot]


[^halot]: HALOT, 236–37.
