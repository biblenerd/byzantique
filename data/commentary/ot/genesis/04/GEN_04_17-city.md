God placed man in a garden, not in a city. It is notable that the first city is built by a murderer (or his son) who was alienated from God. This is perhap a critique of the ANE understanding of how various heroes of old brought technology from the gods to humanity to instantiate urban civilation (depicted through the discoveries / skills of Cain's ancestors),[^note] which will culminate in [ch. 11](ref:GEN 11) with the construction in Babel. St. Augustine uses Cain as the basis for his earthly city (as opposed to the city of God in exile on earth).[^aug]


[^note]: See [note on Genesis 4:20–22](note:GEN_04_20-22-technology).
[^aug]: *Cf., e.g.,* St. Augustine, *City of God* 15.15.
